## Decibels as a absolute measurement

### dBm
is an absolute unit measuring signal power relative to one milliwatt, where 0 dBm equals 1 mW. It is used in telecom to track exact transmitter strength (positive values) and received signal degradation (negative values).

### dBu
is a relative-turned-absolute unit measuring voltage relative to a fixed baseline of 0.775 RMS Volts (0 dBu = 0.775 V). It is the industry standard in professional audio and telecommunications for calibrating equipment levels, independent of circuit impedance.

<img width="862" height="143" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/4029c1fb-fbbe-41bf-9cf9-625762a33b81" />

### dBV
is an absolute unit measuring voltage relative to a fixed baseline of 1 RMS Volt (0 dBV = 1 V). It is the industry standard for consumer audio equipment, microphones, and sensors to quantify exact electrical signal amplitudes.
