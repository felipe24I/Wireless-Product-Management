## Modulation
* **Modulation** is a process of mixing a signal with a sinusoid to produce a new signal increasing the frecuency (frecuency shift). This new signal, will have certain benefits over an unmodulated signal (immute to noise, its more efficient, HF signal allows to decrease the size of the antenna)
* The sinusoidal signal that is used in the modulation is known as the **carrier signal**, or simply "the carrier (high frequency)". The signal that is used in modulating the carrier signal is known as the "data signal" or the "message signal".

 ### Modulation Types
 #### Analog
 * **AM:** Amplitude modulation
 * **FM:** Frequency Modulation
 * **PM:** Phase Modulation

#### Digital
* **ASK:** Amplitude Shift Keying
* **FSK:** Frequency Shift Keying
* **PSK:** Phase Shift Keying

### Amplitude Modulation (AM)
* In Amplitude Modulation or AM, the carrier signal has its amplitude modulated in proportion to the message bearing (lower frequency) or data signal

<img width="1157" height="320" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/8f9601ce-d70c-4a60-ab70-3165911df7d0" />

* AM signal has a constant frequency, but its amplitude is changing all the time and this change is proportional to our information signal

### Frequency Modulation (FM)
* We can define the FM wave, produced when we modulate a carrier frequency fc with modulating signal
* We have a constant amplitude and frequency is changing
* The change of frequency is proportional to our data
* When the amplitude of our data is high we have hig frequency in FM signal and when the signal is decreasing, the frequency decreases in FM signal

### Phase Modulation (PM)
* Phase modulation (PM) is the process of modulating the phase of the carrier
* Amplitude constant, Frequency constant, phase changing all time
* Phase is changing proportional to our data
