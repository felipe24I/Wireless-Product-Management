## Microwave link
* A Microwave link is a communication system that uses a beam of radio waves in the microwave frequency range to transmit information between two fixed locations on the earth
* Microwave links are so adaptable is that they are broadband. That means they can move large amounts of information at high speeds
* Microwaves are also able to penetarte rain, fog, and snow, which means bad weather doesn't disrupt transmission

### Components
* Transmitter: produces a microwave signal that carries the information to be communicated
* Receiver
* Transmission lines
* antennas

* **1. Trasmitter jobs:** Generating microwave energy at the required frequency and power level and modulating
* **2. Transmission line:** This line carries the signal from the transmitter to the antenna and, at the receiving end of the link, from the antenna to the receiver. (Pwer lines, telephone wires and speaker cable are common transmission lines
* **3. Antennas:**
* 1. On the transmitting end, the antenna emits the microwave signal from the transmission line into free space
* 2. At the receiver site, an **antenna** pointed toward the transmitting station **collects the signal energy** and feeds it into the transmission line for processing by the receiver
* **Note:** **Antennas used in microwave links are highly directional**, which means they tightky focus the transmitted energy, and receive energy mainly from one specific direction
* **4. Receiver:** Here, information from the microwave signal is extracted and made available in its original form, the receiver must demodulate the signal to separate the information from the microwave energy that carries it. The receiver must be capable of detecting very small amounts of microwave energy, because the signal loses mcuh of its strength on its journey

### Frequency Planning
The design and construction of a microwave radio link network is based on a number of factors. These include:

* Distance between microwave radio terminals.
* Frequency of operation.
* Interference management to the microwave link receiver.
* Fading, dispersion and multipath distortion.
* Size of antennas, feedline properties, need for towers and masts, and for high gain antennas.
* Management of moisture inside external waveguides.
* Management of equipment, power and security alarms, remote control switching and order wire systems.
* Cost of equipment and cost benefit analysis including equipment maintenance.
