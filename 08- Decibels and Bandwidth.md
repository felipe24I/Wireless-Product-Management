## Decibels

* **Direct measurement:** evaluates a quantity straight from observation using a physical instrument, without requiring calculations or mathematical formulas
* **Indirect measurement:** is the process of determining the value of a unknown quantity by measuring related, accessible quantities and applying mathematical principles, rather than measuring the target object directly
* **Relative measurement:** is the process of quantifying a value by comparing it to a specific reference point, rather than using a fixed or absolute unit (like inches, kilograms, or volts)
* **Absolute measurement:** is the process of quantifying a value using a fixed, standardized unit that does not change based on context or reference points

* **dB (decibel):** is a logarithmic unit used to express the ratio between two values of a physical quantity, most commonly acoustic intensity or electrical power. It's used to express the gain or loss (attenuation) of a signal as it passes through a system.

<img width="753" height="77" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/a833a58c-137f-4429-806d-5fea7ed879ce" />

* **Gain:** is a relative measurement that quantifies how much a system or an amplifier increases the strength of a signal. It represents the ratio of the output signal to the input signal.

* **Loss (attenuation):** is a relative measurement that quantifies how much a signal weakens as it passes through a system, cable, or medium. It represents the ratio of the input signal to the output signal. Mathematically, loss is just negative gain (a system gain of -3 dB means a signal loss of 3 dB.)

* **Unity Gain (0 dB):** When a system has a gain of 0 dB, it is called unity gain. This means the output signal is exactly equal to the input signal (\(P_{\text{out}} = P_{\text{in}}\)). The system is neither amplifying (gaining) nor attenuating (losing) the signal strength.
* **the 3 dB point (or half-power point):** is defined as the threshold where the power of an electrical, radio, or optical signal is exactly doubled (+3 dB) or halved (-3 dB) relative to a reference level.
* **Positive 3 dB (+3 dB):** A doubling of the total transmitted power (\(P_{\text{out}} = 2 \times P_{\text{in}}\)). power ratio is 2:1
* **Negative 3 dB (-3 dB):** A loss of exactly 50% of the signal power (\(P_{\text{out}} = 0.5 \times P_{\text{in}}\)). power ratio is 1:2

* **Analog Bandwidth:** is defined as the width of the usable frequency range of a system or component, measured between the two points where the signal power drops to exactly half of its peak value (the -3 dB points).
* **Digital Bandwidth (or Data Rate / Transmission Speed):** The data transmission speed of a digital system. It is expressed in bps (bits per second).
